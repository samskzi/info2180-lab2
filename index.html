Is Hair Porosity freaking you out too?

October 1, 2020 by Samara Soares



Listen hair porosity is the most important thing that a black woman can learn about. Once you learn the porosity of you hair, you'll suddenly understand why products work or don't work for your hair type, and you'll spend far less money on boatloads of products that simply don't work. So what IS hair porosity? Well hair porosity refers to how well your hair absorbs and retains moisture. This is decided by the cuticle of your hair which decides which determines how easily moisture passes in and out of your hair strands. You have three main types of porosity. There is:

  • High porosity - your hair very easily absorbs and retains moisture, but also very easily loses moisture
  • Normal Porosity - the rate at which your absorbs and retains moisture is moderate
  • Low Porosity - your hair doesn't easily allow moisture to be absorbed in your hair shaft

A simple way to test your porosity is to drop a dry strand of hair into a glass of water, if it sinks rapidly, you have high porosity hair, if it floats at the top, you have low porosity hair and if it steadily floats from the top to the bottom, your hair is considered normal porosity. Find out your porosity now, and we'll talk more next week. See ya!

The Grand Debate on Hair Types

September 23, 2020 by Samara Soares



There is a large debate in the natural hair community around if hair types are accurate and if "hair typing" should actually be a thing. It's up to you to decide whether you agree or disagree with the practice, but for me, I think it's neither here nor there. Hair Type does not matter, as you and someone can have virtually the same hair TYPE but find that products that work for you won't work for them and vice versa. It may be an easier way to categorize black women, but it's definetely not accurate, since many persons have many different curl patterns displayed through their hair. Nonetheless, there are 4 different hair types, 1,2,3 and 4, and within those different groups, you can be A through to C. eg: 4A, 3B, 2A. Types 1 and 2 are generally straighter hair, with extremely loose curl patterns, while types 3 and 4 have tighter curl patterns and more pronounced curl definition. Do some research and let me know, what's your hair type?
